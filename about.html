About us

Money by the people, for the people.

OmniTender Systems LLC helps independent operators accept more payment types with transparent pricing and direct human support — payment by the people, for the people, rooted in Western North Carolina.

What we do

We are a merchant payment exchange and payment services company — not procurement software and not a crypto trading app. Customers pay in supported methods at checkout; conversion happens once; merchants receive settlement in their bank. We onboard merchants, configure POS and checkout flows, and support you after go-live. Processing and settlement run on licensed partner rails; OmniTender never holds merchant or customer funds.

Who we serve

  • Restaurants, cafés, and food retailers
  • Retail and boutiques with seasonal volume spikes
  • Service businesses and trades that want crypto-capable checkout without volatility risk
  • Grocers and markets pursuing EBT/SNAP (with merchant FNS authorization)

How we're different

  • Transparent pricing — wholesale-style visibility instead of opaque tiers
  • Breadth — cards, debit, crypto, and EBT on one relationship
  • Direct support — reach a real person at (828) 413-0859
  • Local roots — built in Morganton, NC for merchants who can't afford fee leakage

Before you reach out

General information only — not a quote or approval guarantee.

Is OmniTender a payment processor?

No. Licensed partner processors and acquirers handle card settlement and compliance. OmniTender helps you apply, compare programs, configure checkout, and get support — we are not the bank or processor of record.

Does OmniTender hold merchant funds?

No. Processing and settlement run on licensed partner rails. OmniTender never holds merchant or customer funds.

Where are you based?

OmniTender Systems LLC is headquartered in Morganton, NC — serving independent merchants across Burke County, Western North Carolina, and beyond. See our Western NC page for local context.

How do I get started?

Request a free rate analysis to compare your current processing, or submit the merchant application when you're ready to explore onboarding. Either path starts a conversation — no obligation until you accept partner terms.
