# Collections Playbook — When a Client Doesn't Pay

**Goal:** Get paid in full with minimum delay. Preserve LLC liability shield. Build a paper trail that holds up in court.

---

## Prevention (do this on every assignment)

1. **Executed MSA + SOW** before day 1.
2. **Weekly signed timesheets** — supervisor signature on hospital letterhead or official email approval.
3. **Invoice within 48 hours** of period end; reference SOW number and PO.
4. **Email invoice** to billing contact + cc contracts/procurement; request read receipt.
5. **Log every dollar** in accounting software; note due date + late-fee date.

If you skip timesheet sign-off, you are negotiating from weakness.

---

## Aging schedule

| Days past due | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| **0** | Invoice sent; due per MSA (default Net 15) |
| **+3** | Friendly email: "Confirm receipt of Invoice #___" |
| **+7** | Phone call to AP contact; document date/time/name |
| **+10** | Formal email citing MSA § payment; late fee begins accruing |
| **+15** | **Suspend services** notice (if still on assignment) per MSA |
| **+20** | [Demand letter](./templates/11-collection-demand-letter.md) via email + certified mail |
| **+30** | Attorney demand letter on law firm letterhead |
| **+45** | File suit or refer to collections (see below) |
| **+60+** | Judgment enforcement — garnishment where available (often limited for hospitals) |

---

## Step 1 — Internal triage (Day 7)

Answer these before escalating:

| Question | Why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Is the invoice addressed correctly (LLC legal name, EIN)? | Wrong vendor ID = endless AP delay |
| Was a PO required and listed? | Hospitals reject non-PO invoices |
| Did timesheets match invoice hours? | #1 dispute reason |
| Is there a "disputed" email from client? | Respond in writing within 2 business days |
| Is client a government entity? | NC Prompt Pay Act / federal rules may help |

**Fix administrative blocks first.** 40% of delays are paperwork, not bad faith.

---

## Step 2 — Suspension of services (Day 15+)

If you are still on site and unpaid for **prior completed periods**:

1. Send written notice per MSA: unpaid invoices #___; services suspend in **48 hours** unless payment received.
2. **Do not** abandon patients mid-shift without supervisor handoff — finish scheduled shift if safety requires, then suspend.
3. Document handoff; keep copies.

Suspension is your strongest non-litigation lever. Hospitals staff labs 24/7 — they feel pain fast.

---

## Step 3 — Demand letter (Day 20)

Use [template](./templates/11-collection-demand-letter.md). Include:

- Total due (principal + contractual late fees + stop-work costs if applicable)
- Invoice numbers and dates
- MSA payment section citation
- **10-day cure period**
- Statement that attorney fees will be sought under MSA
- Send **certified mail + email** to AP and contracts signatory

---

## Step 4 — Attorney involvement (Day 30)

Give counsel:

- Executed MSA + SOW
- Invoices + delivery proof
- Timesheets
- Email thread
- COI / proof you performed work (badge logs, schedule emails)

**Likely paths:**

| Path | Best when |
|------|-----------|
| **Demand letter + negotiation** | Client is slow AP, not insolvent |
| **NC Superior Court breach of contract** | Clear amount, <$25K or within court limits; strong docs |
| **Arbitration** | Only if MSA requires — you chose litigation default in our template |
| **Commercial collections agency** | You want hands-off; expect 25–40% contingency |
| **Mechanic's lien** | **Generally NOT available** for professional MLS labor in NC — do not rely on this |

---

## Step 5 — Litigation realities (be eyes-open)

| Factor | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| **Hospitals have lawyers** | They will fight over timesheet disputes |
| **Time to judgment** | Months to years depending on court |
| **Collecting judgment** | Non-profit hospitals may have limited garnishment targets; still worth judgment for credit/reporting |
| **Your LLC shield** | Keep all conduct in LLC name; no commingling |
| **Attorney fees clause** | Mutual prevailing-party fees in MSA — critical |

**Small claims:** NC Magistrate court limit is **$10,000** (as of 2024 — verify current). May work for single invoice; hospitals often remove to Superior Court.

## Dispute types & responses

### "Hours weren't approved"

**Response:** Produce signed timesheet or email approval. MSA **deemed approval** clause after 5 business days silence.

### "Quality / performance issues"

**Response:** MSA requires **written notice and cure period** before offset. Client cannot unilaterally withhold payment for undocumented claims. Offer good-faith review; do not admit liability without insurer.

### "We never signed that rate"

**Response:** Produce executed SOW. If only verbal — lesson learned; settlement negotiation.

### "Invoice doesn't match PO"

**Response:** Get PO amended retroactively or issue credit/rebill. AP machines stall on mismatch.

### "You're really an employee — tax issue"

**Response:** IRS classification is separate from payment obligation. They still owe for services rendered under **quantum meruit** if no contract — but get attorney.

---

## Credit reporting (B2B)

Some agencies report commercial delinquency (e.g., Dun & Bradstreet Paydex). Discuss with collections counsel — hospital vendors care about vendor score.

---

## Write-off decision

Consider writing off only when:

- Legal cost exceeds recoverable amount **and**
- No partial payment likely **and**
- Attorney advises

Document write-off with CPA for tax loss treatment.

---

## After you win

- Update client **do-not-work** list
- Require **prepayment** or **irrevocable letter of credit** for any future engagement (rare but possible)
- Share experience (without defamation) in MLS contractor networks — hospitals that stiff vendors get known
