## RECITALS

A. Client operates one or more healthcare facilities and requires qualified clinical laboratory professionals on a temporary or contingent basis.

B. Contractor provides professional clinical laboratory staffing services through licensed, certified personnel, including services performed by **[PRINCIPAL NAME]**, ASCP-certified Medical Laboratory Scientist ("**Professional**").

C. The Parties desire to set forth the general terms under which Contractor will provide Services, with specific assignments to be documented in Statements of Work.

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1 "Services"** means professional clinical laboratory testing support, specimen processing, instrument operation, quality control, result reporting, and related tasks performed by Professional in accordance with Client policies, applicable law, and the scope of a Statement of Work.

**1.2 "Statement of Work" or "SOW"** means a written order executed by both Parties substantially in the form of Exhibit B, describing a specific assignment.

**1.3 "Fees"** means amounts payable for Services at the rates and terms set forth in the applicable SOW and Exhibit D.

**1.4 "Confidential Information"** means non-public information disclosed by either Party, including patient information (PHI), business, financial, technical, and operational information.

**1.5 "PHI"** has the meaning in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

---

## 2. SERVICES

**2.1 Performance.** Contractor shall perform Services professionally and in accordance with applicable federal and state law, CLIA regulations (under Client's certificate of compliance), Client laboratory policies, and each SOW.

**2.2 Statement of Work.** Each assignment requires a fully executed SOW. If a SOW conflicts with this Agreement, the SOW controls **only** for that assignment and **only** to the extent of the conflict.

**2.3 No Work Without Paper.** Contractor has **no obligation** to perform Services and Professional shall **not** report to Client premises until this Agreement, any required Business Associate Agreement, and the applicable SOW are fully executed and credentialing clearance is complete.

**2.4 Professional of Record.** Unless a SOW states otherwise, Professional performing Services is **[PRINCIPAL NAME]**, ASCP MLS #[**NUMBER**], licensed in **[STATE(S)]**.

---

## 3. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR RELATIONSHIP

**3.1 Status.** Contractor is an **independent contractor**, not an employee, agent, joint venturer, or partner of Client. Nothing herein creates an employment relationship between Client and Contractor or Professional.

**3.2 Control.** Client may specify clinical standards, shift times, and outcomes required for patient care. Client shall **not** control the **manner and means** by which Contractor accomplishes professional laboratory work except as required by law, accreditation standards, and reasonable facility safety rules. Exhibit A supplements this section.

**3.3 No Benefits.** Client owes no employee benefits, workers' compensation (except as required by law or separate certificate), unemployment insurance, or withholding for Contractor or Professional.

**3.4 Taxes.** Contractor is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from payments under this Agreement.

**3.5 Other Clients.** Contractor may provide services to other clients, subject to conflict-of-interest disclosures and scheduling commitments in an active SOW.

---

## 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

**4.1 Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for **two (2) years**, automatically renewing for successive one-year terms unless either Party gives **sixty (60) days'** written notice of non-renewal.

**4.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement without cause on **thirty (30) days'** written notice, except that active SOWs remain in effect until completed or terminated under Section 4.3.

**4.3 Termination of SOW.** Either Party may terminate an active SOW on **fourteen (14) days'** written notice. Client may terminate an SOW immediately for **material breach** by Contractor after **written notice and five (5) business days** to cure (if curable). Contractor may terminate an SOW immediately if Client fails to pay undisputed Fees when due after notice under Section 5.5.

**4.4 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Client shall pay all **undisputed Fees** for Services performed through the termination date, approved expenses, and any cancellation fee stated in the SOW. Sections 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13 survive termination.

**4.5 Early Cancellation by Client.** If Client terminates an SOW for convenience with less than fourteen (14) days' notice, or cancels scheduled guaranteed hours within fourteen (14) days of start, Client shall pay Contractor a cancellation fee equal to **fifty percent (50%)** of the remaining **guaranteed hours** in the SOW at the contracted rate, **or** the minimum cancellation fee in the SOW if higher.

---

## 5. COMPENSATION, INVOICING, AND NON-PAYMENT REMEDIES

**5.1 Fees.** Client shall pay Fees at the rates in the applicable SOW and Exhibit D.

**5.2 Invoicing.** Contractor shall invoice **[weekly / biweekly]** for Services performed in the prior period. Each invoice shall include: SOW number, PO number (if any), dates of service, hours by category (regular, OT, holiday), rate, and total due.

**5.3 Payment Terms.** Client shall pay undisputed invoices within **fifteen (15) calendar days** of receipt ("**Net 15**") by ACH or wire to Contractor's designated account.

**5.4 Late Payment.** Overdue amounts accrue interest at **one and one-half percent (1.5%) per month** (eighteen percent (18%) per annum), or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is less, from the due date until paid.

**5.5 Right to Suspend.** If Client fails to pay any **undisputed** invoice within **ten (10) calendar days** after written notice, Contractor may **immediately suspend Services** under any active SOW without liability. Suspension does not waive Contractor's right to payment.

**5.6 Acceleration.** If Client breaches Section 5 or fails to cure payment within **fifteen (15) days** after written demand, **all unpaid Fees** under all SOWs become **immediately due and payable**.

**5.7 No Setoff.** Client may not withhold, reduce, or set off payment for alleged counterclaims unless (a) undisputed and admitted in writing by Contractor, or (b) finally determined by a court of competent jurisdiction. Performance disputes do **not** excuse timely payment of **undisputed** portions.

**5.8 Timesheet Approval.** Client shall designate a supervisor to verify hours on Contractor's timesheet form (Exhibit B attachment). Client must dispute hours in writing within **five (5) business days** of receipt; otherwise hours are **deemed approved**.

**5.9 Expenses.** Pre-approved travel, parking, and required credentialing costs are reimbursed at cost with receipts unless the SOW states otherwise.

**5.10 Exhibit E.** Non-payment remedies in Exhibit E are incorporated by reference.

## 6. INSURANCE

**6.1 Contractor Coverage.** Contractor shall maintain insurance per Exhibit C throughout the Term and any SOW performance period.

**6.2 Certificates.** Contractor shall provide certificates of insurance upon request. Client shall be named additional insured on CGL only as Exhibit C provides.

**6.3 Notice of Cancellation.** Contractor's insurer shall endeavor to provide thirty (30) days' notice of cancellation or material change.

---

## 7. HIPAA AND COMPLIANCE

**7.1 Business Associate.** If Contractor or Professional accesses PHI, the Parties shall execute a Business Associate Agreement ("**BAA**") substantially in the form of Exhibit HIPAA (or Client's form if substantively equivalent, per attorney review).

**7.2 Laws.** Each Party shall comply with applicable law, including HIPAA, CLIA (Client as lab entity), OSHA, and anti-kickback/stark rules as applicable.

**7.3 Credentialing.** Contractor shall complete Client's reasonable credentialing per Exhibit Credentialing. Client shall not unreasonably delay clearance.

---

## 8. CONFIDENTIALITY

**8.1 Obligations.** Each Party shall protect the other's Confidential Information using at least reasonable care and no less than the care used for its own sensitive information.

**8.2 PHI.** PHI shall be handled only per the BAA and applicable law.

**8.3 Return.** Upon termination, Contractor shall return or destroy Confidential Information per Client policy and BAA, except records retained for legal, tax, or insurance purposes.

---

## 9. INDEMNIFICATION AND LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

**9.1 By Contractor.** Contractor shall indemnify Client against third-party claims arising from Contractor's or Professional's **gross negligence or willful misconduct** in performing Services, subject to Professional's insurance.

**9.2 By Client.** Client shall indemnify Contractor against third-party claims arising from (a) Client's facility conditions, (b) Client's directions violating law, or (c) Client's **gross negligence or willful misconduct**, except to the extent caused by Contractor.

**9.3 Professional Liability.** Contractor's professional liability insurer shall be primary for claims arising from laboratory professional services within scope.

**9.4 Cap.** Except for indemnification obligations, breach of confidentiality/PHI, or fraud, each Party's aggregate liability under this Agreement shall not exceed **Fees paid or payable** under the SOW giving rise to the claim during the **twelve (12) months** preceding the claim.

**9.5 No Consequential Damages.** Neither Party is liable for indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages, except for breach of PHI obligations.

---

## 10. NON-CIRCUMVENTION

During the Term and for **twelve (12) months** thereafter, Client shall not engage Professional **directly** for laboratory services at Client facilities **without** compensating Contractor per a mutually agreed buyout fee of **fifteen percent (15%)** of Professional's anticipated first-year compensation or **$25,000**, whichever is greater, unless this Agreement is terminated for Contractor's uncured material breach.

## 11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

**11.1 Negotiation.** Disputes shall first be escalated to each Party's designated contact for good-faith negotiation within **ten (10) business days**.

**11.2 Mediation.** If unresolved in **thirty (30) days**, either Party may request non-binding mediation in **[Burke County, NC / Charlotte, NC]** before litigation.

**11.3 Litigation.** Either Party may pursue litigation in the courts specified in Section 12. **Nothing prevents Contractor from seeking injunctive relief or accounts receivable judgment** for non-payment.

**11.4 Attorney Fees.** In any action to enforce this Agreement, the **prevailing Party** shall recover **reasonable attorneys' fees, costs, and expenses**, including collection costs.

## 12. GOVERNING LAW AND VENUE

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the **State of North Carolina**, without regard to conflicts principles. Exclusive venue lies in the **state courts of Burke County, North Carolina**, or the **U.S. District Court for the Western District of North Carolina**, and each Party consents to personal jurisdiction therein.
