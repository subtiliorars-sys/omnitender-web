## 1. BACKGROUND

Covered Entity is a HIPAA covered entity. Business Associate provides clinical laboratory professional services that may require access to Protected Health Information ("**PHI**"). This BAA complies with 45 C.F.R. Parts 160 and 164 (HIPAA, HITECH).

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

Terms not defined here have meanings in HIPAA. **PHI**, **Protected Health Information**, **Security Incident**, **Breach**, and **Designated Record Set** have meanings in 45 C.F.R. § 160.103.

---

## 3. PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES

**3.1** Business Associate may use and disclose PHI **only** to perform Services under the MSA, as permitted by this BAA, or as required by law.

**3.2** Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI in a manner that would violate Subpart E of 45 C.F.R. Part 164 if done by Covered Entity, except as permitted in Section 3.1.

**3.3** Business Associate may use PHI for proper management and administration of Business Associate or to carry out legal responsibilities, provided disclosures are required by law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from recipient.

---

## 4. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

Business Associate shall:

**4.1** Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards reasonably designed to protect PHI per 45 C.F.R. § 164.308, .310, .312.

**4.2** Ensure agents/subcontractors with PHI access agree to same restrictions (written agreement).

**4.3** Report to Covered Entity **without unreasonable delay** and no later than **ten (10) calendar days** after discovery of:
- any **Security Incident** involving PHI
- any **Breach** of Unsecured PHI per 45 C.F.R. § 164.410

**4.4** Make internal practices, books, and records available to HHS for compliance determination.

**4.5** Make PHI in a Designated Record Set available to Covered Entity for individual access requests per 45 C.F.R. § 164.524 (within **ten (10) business days** of Covered Entity request).

**4.6** Amend PHI per 45 C.F.R. § 164.526 when directed by Covered Entity.

**4.7** Document and make available information required for accounting of disclosures per 45 C.F.R. § 164.528.

**4.8** Return or destroy PHI at termination per Section 7, if feasible.

---

## 5. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

Covered Entity shall:

**5.1** Provide Notice of Privacy Practices and permissions/authorizations where required.

**5.2** Not request Business Associate to use/disclose PHI in violation of HIPAA.

**5.3** Notify Business Associate of restrictions on PHI use that affect Services.

---

## 6. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES BY BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

Business Associate may disclose PHI:

- As required by law, with notice to Covered Entity if permitted
- For health oversight, abuse investigations, or judicial proceedings as HIPAA permits
- To report violations of law to appropriate authorities

---

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

**7.1** Term matches the MSA.

**7.2** Covered Entity may terminate if Business Associate materially breaches and fails to cure within **thirty (30) days** of notice (if curable).

**7.3** Upon termination, Business Associate shall return or destroy PHI where feasible; if not feasible, extend protections per 45 C.F.R. § 164.504(e)(2)(ii)(J).

---

## 8. BREACH COOPERATION

Business Associate shall cooperate with Covered Entity's breach notification obligations, including providing information for risk assessment within **five (5) business days** of request.

---

## 9. LIABILITY

Breach of this BAA is a material breach of the MSA. Limitation of liability in MSA **does not apply** to breaches of PHI obligations caused by Business Associate's negligence or willful misconduct.
