# OmniTender Systems LLC — Clinical Laboratory Staffing Foundation

**Purpose:** Legal, operational, and collections infrastructure for contracting ASCP-certified Medical Laboratory Scientist (MLS) services to hospitals and medical entities through OmniTender Systems LLC.

**Entity:** OmniTender Systems LLC (Morganton, North Carolina)

**Suggested DBA (file with NC Secretary of State / county as required):**  
`OmniTender Clinical Laboratory Services` or `OmniTender Laboratory Staffing`

---

## Important disclaimer

These materials are **contract templates and operational checklists**, not legal advice. Healthcare contracting, independent-contractor classification, HIPAA, state licensure, and hospital vendor rules vary by state and facility.

**Before signing your first client agreement:**

1. Have a **North Carolina business/healthcare attorney** review and customize templates for your situation.
2. Confirm whether your LLC needs a **DBA**, separate **E&O/professional liability** policy, and any **state staffing or professional** registrations.
3. Confirm **IRS independent-contractor classification** with your CPA or attorney (hospitals sometimes push W-2 through a staffing intermediary).

## Recommended signing order (per client)

```
1. Mutual NDA (optional, if negotiations are pre-MSA)
2. Master Services Agreement (MSA) + Exhibits A–E
3. HIPAA Business Associate Agreement (if PHI access)
4. Statement of Work / Assignment Order (per placement)
5. W-9 (IRS form — not in this repo; use current IRS PDF)
6. Certificate(s) of Insurance (COI) from your broker
7. Credentialing packet per Exhibit C
```

**Rule:** Do not start work until **MSA + SOW are fully executed** and, if applicable, **credentialing clearance** is documented in writing.

## Core protections (why these documents exist)

| Risk | Protection built into templates |
|------|----------------------------------|
| Client doesn't pay | Net-15 terms, 1.5%/month late fee, right to **suspend services**, **acceleration** of all amounts due, **prevailing-party attorney fees**, interest on judgments |
| Client disputes hours worked | **Timesheet verification** with supervisor signature; deemed approval if no dispute within 5 business days |
| Client treats you like an employee | **Independent Contractor Exhibit**; LLC invoices; no benefits; right to serve other clients |
| Client claims malpractice | **Professional liability insurance** requirement; mutual indemnification scoped to negligence |
| PHI breach allegation | **HIPAA BAA** with required safeguards and breach notice |
| Client poaches and cuts you out | Limited **non-circumvention** during term + 12 months (attorney to tune) |
| Weak paper trail | SOW per assignment, numbered invoices, retained timesheets, email notices clause |

## Business infrastructure (non-contract)

Complete these in parallel with legal docs:

- [ ] **EIN** — separate from SSN for LLC banking/invoicing
- [ ] **Business bank account** — all client payments to LLC only
- [ ] **Accounting** — QuickBooks/Wave; accrual or cash per CPA advice
- [ ] **Professional liability (E&O)** — $1M/$3M minimum for hospital lab work
- [ ] **General liability** — $1M per occurrence typical vendor requirement
- [ ] **Cyber liability** — recommended if accessing hospital EMR remotely
- [ ] **Workers' compensation** — NC may require for LLC members in some structures; hospitals often require proof or exemption certificate
- [ ] **State MLS licensure** — active in every state you perform patient testing
- [ ] **ASCP certification** — current; copy in credentialing file
- [ ] **Malpractice tail** — if you ever carried individual policy
- [ ] **Contract-review attorney** — one-time template review + phone consult retainer
- [ ] **Collections counsel** — identify firm before you need them

## Converting templates to signed PDFs

1. Copy template to Word/Google Docs.
2. Fill `[BRACKETED]` fields.
3. Attorney review.
4. Export PDF; use **DocuSign**, **Adobe Sign**, or wet signature.
5. Store executed copies: `contracts/[ClientName]/MSA-[date].pdf`

## Questions for your attorney (bring this list)

1. Is direct 1099 lab contracting enforceable with target hospitals, or must we use a staffing intermediary?
2. Does OmniTender need a **staffing agency license** in NC or client states?
3. Are our **non-circumvention** and **non-solicitation** clauses reasonable under NC law?
4. Should we use **Burke County** venue exclusively or allow federal court?
5. Is **1.5%/month** late interest enforceable and below NC usury caps for this contract type?
6. Do we need a **personal guaranty from the hospital** (rare) or **prepaid retainer** for new clients?
7. HIPAA BAA — is subcontractor language needed if you later add bench techs?
